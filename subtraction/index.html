Subtraction

/səbˈtrakʃ(ə)n/

−

a − b pulls you back. Start at zero, step out to a, then step back by b. Where you land is the answer. Unlike addition, order matters — 5 − 2 is not 2 − 5.

On a number line

First vector goes to a. Second vector reverses direction and pulls back by b. Swap a and b with the sliders to feel why order matters.

Always numeric

Where + hedges — if either side is a string, it glues instead of adds — − never does. Minus always tries to coerce both sides to numbers. Anything that can't become a number comes out as NaN.
